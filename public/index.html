About the Program

The SSR Program is a student-led civic engagement initiative designed to promote ethical leadership, social consciousness, and active citizenship among high school students in the International High School (IHS). By connecting academic learning with real-world action, students develop the ability to think critically, act compassionately, and lead responsibly.

Purpose & Rationale

The NFLSXL IHS Student Social Responsibility (SSR) Program was created to move education beyond exams and rankings. It gives students a structured platform to explore what it means to be a responsible global citizen, to understand the needs of their communities, and to transform concern into meaningful action.

Program Objectives

  • Equip students with knowledge and skills to address social, environmental, and ethical issues.
  • Foster long-term partnerships with local and global organizations.
  • Encourage meaningful community service and project-based learning.
  • Build student confidence, leadership, and collaboration through real-world challenges.

Core Pillars: L · S · L

  • Learn – Investigate social, environmental, and ethical issues through academic and co-curricular learning.
  • Serve – Participate in community-based volunteer projects and outreach programs.
  • Lead – Initiate and manage student-driven campaigns, events, and partnerships.

Target Participants

  • High school students in IHS (Grades 10–12) who are interested in social responsibility and leadership.
  • Faculty members who serve as mentors and advisors for student projects.
  • Local community partners and international collaborators who work with students on authentic challenges.

Expected Outcomes

  • Increased student awareness of and engagement with real-world issues.
  • Stronger ties between NFLSXL and the wider Nanjing community.
  • Enhanced student portfolios for university and scholarship applications.
  • Growth in student confidence, leadership, empathy, and communication.

Program Timeline (2025–2026)

  • Phase 1 – Launch (Sept 1–15, 2025): Website publication, program orientation, and student sign-ups.
  • Phase 2 – Projects Begin (Sept–April 2026): Weekly team meetings, fieldwork, and campaign development.

Key Projects

SSR projects include community outreach, environmental action, and international collaboration. Each project strengthens leadership, creativity, and civic awareness among students.

Signature Projects

  • Community Outreach in Nanjing – Visits and long-term partnerships with elderly homes, migrant worker schools, and underserved communities; initiatives on waste reduction and neighborhood improvement.
  • SSR Annual Conference – A year-end showcase where students present their projects, share impact stories, and engage with guest speakers from education, NGOs, and industry.
  • SSR Awareness Campaigns – Student-led campaigns on mental health, gender equity, digital safety, climate justice, and other urgent social issues.
  • Global Citizenship Projects – Collaborations with international schools, NGOs, and online conferences to explore global challenges and cross-cultural solutions.

How Projects Work

  • Students form project teams based on shared interests (e.g., environment, education, public health).
  • Each team works with a faculty advisor to design a project plan, set measurable goals, and identify community partners.
  • Teams meet weekly for research, planning, and reflection, combining classroom learning with fieldwork.
  • Throughout the year, students collect data, document their work, and prepare presentations and digital portfolios.

Assessment & Evaluation

  • Student reflection logs and journals to track learning and personal growth.
  • Monthly advisor reviews focusing on leadership, teamwork, and project progress.
  • Feedback from community partners on impact and collaboration.
  • Final rubric-based assessment of communication, initiative, and social impact, often used as part of student portfolios.

Sustainability & Continuity

  • Annual handover from graduating leaders to younger peers through an SSR student council/committee structure.
  • Training and onboarding for new faculty advisors each academic year.
  • Ongoing web updates and digital archiving of projects to inspire future cohorts.

Support Infrastructure

  • Faculty Roles – Advisors guide students on project design, ethical practice, and impact evaluation. SSR themes are integrated into subjects such as Psychology, English, and Social Studies.
  • Student Leadership – An SSR student council helps coordinate projects, organize events, and support communication with partners.

Tools & Platforms

  • SSR Website – A dedicated platform (dark mode, interactive layouts, and sign-up forms) for sharing updates, stories, and project results.
  • Social Media – Channels such as Instagram, YouTube, and WeChat are used to promote campaigns and highlight student work.
  • Digital Publications – Annual reports and online portfolios document student projects and community impact.

Partnerships

  • Local – Community centers, NGOs, schools for underserved populations, and other groups in Nanjing who collaborate on outreach, education, and service projects.
  • Global – International schools, nonprofit organizations, and online conferences that provide a platform for global citizenship and intercultural dialogue.
